What is this scan?

Flux Forward works with internationals navigating the Dutch market. Over time, we identified 5 specific layers where progress tends to slow down. This scan maps which one may be relevant to your situation — and gives you a specific next step.

The 5 layers
1
Priority Confusion
Too many directions at once, no clear decision structure.
2
System Blindness
The system is still partly invisible — effort lands in the obvious places but not always the effective ones.
3
Positioning Collapse
Your experience is real, but it's not landing clearly in this context.
4
Execution Fragmentation
Effort is spread too wide to build traction. Each week starts from zero.
5
Capacity Breakdown
Capacity has quietly dropped — pushing harder is making things harder, not better.

Question 1 of 5
I'm unclear about what to focus on right now.
For example: job vs PhD, staying in NL vs moving, short-term income vs long-term direction. Multiple paths feel equally important.
Many people arrive in the Netherlands with a clear goal — then find the context shifts what's possible or desirable. This question looks at whether that's created a decision gap.
What this question looks at
Whether your energy is split across too many directions at once, making it hard to build momentum on any of them.
Question 2 of 5
I mostly rely on LinkedIn or job boards to find opportunities.
I'm not fully sure how the less visible parts of the market work here — referrals, networks, or how hiring actually moves in my field.
The Dutch job market has a visible layer and an invisible one. Most people start with what they can see. This question checks whether the invisible part is still unclear.
What this question looks at
Whether part of the system is still hard to read — not just your effort inside it, but the way it works underneath.
Question 3 of 5
I struggle to explain my experience clearly in this context.
My background feels strong, but I'm not confident that employers or my network here quickly understand what I bring or where I fit.
Moving countries often creates a translation gap — not in language, but in how your value is read. Strong experience doesn't automatically land the same way in a new context.
What this question looks at
Whether the issue is not your skills, but how you present your experience in a way that lands clearly here.
Question 4 of 5
I'm doing many things, but they don't build on each other.
I apply, network, explore options, attend events — but it doesn't feel like momentum is building. Things feel scattered rather than compounding.
Activity and progress are not the same thing. This question looks at whether your effort is concentrated enough to create a loop — or spread too wide to compound.
What this question looks at
Whether the issue is not effort, but execution that's spread too wide to create traction.
Question 5 of 5
I'm running low on energy to keep going.
The uncertainty, pressure, and setbacks have affected my capacity to keep showing up consistently. It takes more effort than it used to.
Long transitions quietly deplete capacity. This isn't a character issue — it's a system issue. When capacity drops, more pressure usually creates more friction, not more progress.
What this question looks at
Whether what looks like lack of progress is actually a capacity problem — and whether pushing harder would help or make it worse.
