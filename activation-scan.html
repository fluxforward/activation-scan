Before you start
What is this scan?

Flux Forward works with internationals navigating the Dutch market. Over time, we identified 5 specific layers where progress tends to slow down. This scan maps which one may be relevant to your situation — and gives you a specific next step.

Who this is for
Internationals in the Netherlands in an active transition — job seeking, changing careers, or navigating the first 1–3 years here. Especially useful when you’re putting in effort but progress feels unclear or slow.
Who this is not for
People who are settled, not in active transition, or not navigating the Dutch market. The questions are designed for a specific context — if that’s not where you are, the results may not be meaningful.
The 5 layers
1
Priority Confusion
Too many directions at once, no clear decision structure.
2
System Blindness
The system is still partly invisible — effort lands in the obvious places but not always the effective ones.
3
Positioning Collapse
Your experience is real, but it's not landing clearly in this context.
4
Execution Fragmentation
Effort is spread too wide to build traction. Each week starts from zero.
5
Capacity Breakdown
Capacity has quietly dropped — pushing harder is making things harder, not better.

Question 1 of 5
In the past two weeks, how many different directions have you actively been pursuing at the same time?
Many people arrive in the Netherlands with a clear goal — then find the context shifts what's possible or desirable. This question looks at whether that's created a decision gap.
Question 2 of 5
When you think about your last 5 opportunities or conversations — how did most of them come about?
The Dutch job market has a visible layer and an invisible one. Most people start with what they can see. This question checks whether the invisible part is still unclear.
Question 3 of 5
When someone asks what you do or what you're looking for here — what usually happens?
Moving countries often creates a translation gap — not in language, but in how your value is read. Strong experience doesn't automatically land the same way in a new context.
Question 4 of 5
Looking at last week — which of these best describes how you spent your energy?
Activity and progress are not the same thing. This question looks at whether your effort is concentrated enough to create a loop — or spread too wide to compound.
Question 5 of 5
When you think about the next step you know you should take — what gets in the way most often?
Long transitions quietly deplete capacity. This isn't a character issue — it's a system issue. When capacity drops, more pressure usually creates more friction, not more progress.
